A New Technique for Measuring a Firm's Marketing Emphasis

Overview

We propose a novel large language model-based technique that uses managers' words in earnings calls to measure marketing emphasis and its components, overcoming limitations of traditional dictionary-based approaches. The dataset provides 254,522 firm-quarter observations from 9,916 U.S. public firms observed from 2003q1 to 2023q1.

Citation

Damavandi, Hoorsana, Mai, Feng, and Astvansh, Vivek (2025). A New Technique for Measuring a Firm's Marketing Emphasis. Marketing Letters.

Measures

Our technique measures a firm's marketing emphasis through 19 lower-order components across three higher-order constructs:

Market Orientation (8 dimensions)

customer orientation, competitor orientation, interfunctional coordination, longterm focus, profit focus, intelligence generation, intelligence dissemination, responsiveness

Marketing Capabilities (8 dimensions)

information management, planning, implementation, pricing capabilities, product development, channel management, communication, selling capabilities

Based on Day 1994; Vorhies & Morgan 2005

Marketing Excellence (3 dimensions)

marketing ecosystem, end user, marketing agility

Based on Homburg, Theel, & Hohenberg 2020; Moorman & Day 2016

Research Applications

Our measures enable researchers to:

  • Study how external events (crises, competitive attacks, acquisitions) affect marketing emphasis
  • Examine the relationship between CEO characteristics and marketing orientation
  • Analyze conversational dynamics between managers and analysts
  • Compare marketing emphasis across industries and time periods
  • Predict firm performance using marketing emphasis as a leading indicator
